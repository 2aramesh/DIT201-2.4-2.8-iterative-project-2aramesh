Rangituhi Walk

Gear List:

Explore the Scenery!

Rangituhi Walk is a scenic adventure through native bush and rolling
hilltops, leading to one of the most breathtaking views in the region. As
you make your way up the well-marked trail, you’ll pass through peaceful
forest, spot birds overhead, and catch glimpses of the harbour far
below. At the summit, you’re rewarded with panoramic views
stretching across Porirua, Kapiti Island, and even the South
Island on a clear day. It’s the perfect mix of nature, challenge,
and fresh air—ideal for stretching your legs and clearing your
head after a busy term.

Key Information:

A private bus will drop students at the start of the Rangituhi walkway. Teachers will help students navigate the path to the lookout and then up to the summit of Rangituhi. From here students will traverse the tops and then pop down into Te Ngahere o Tawa. Students will spend more time on the walk learning about reading maps, trees and leaves, local places of interest from viewpoints etc. There is also a $50 prezzy card as a prize for activities students can complete on the way.

Adrenalin Forest

Gear List:

Fuel the Adrenaline!

Adrenalin Forest a multi-level aerial obstacle course set high in the treetops, designed to test your courage, balance, and physical strength. With rope bridges, swinging logs, wobbly platforms, flying foxes and more, each level pushes you higher off the ground and deeper into the forest canopy. As you progress, the challenges get tougher, and so does the adrenaline rush. Will you stay nice and safe on Level 3, or dare to defy gravity all the way to the heart-pounding heights of Level 6? Do you have the guts to take on the final challenge? This is where fun meets fear — and you walk the line between both.

Key Information:

Students will meet at Tawa Train Station where the roll will be taken. Please gather with your form class for the roll and if you are running late, you must find a teacher and let them know you have arrived. Students will catch the 8:28am train to Porirua and then walk as a group to Adrenalin Forest. We need to catch this earlier train to ensure we start climbing on time.

>>

If Students absolutely cannot make this train (as I realise that it is before the start of school time) there will be one teacher waiting at the train station for any late students and they will catch the 8.48am train. This is an absolute back up and is only an option for those that cannot possibly make it earlier. These students will be late for the start of the climbing and will not get their full 3 hours. If your whanau lives in Porirua and Students will meet us at Adrenalin Forest, please wait at the entrance to Adrenalin Forest and let a teacher know when the group gets there so we can update the roll and let school know you are in our care. After a safety briefing students have 3 hours to climb and test yourselves.

Dive in for a splash!

Te Rauparaha Aquatic Centre is a modern indoor aquatic facility offering a fun and relaxing water experience for all. The centre includes a 25-metre lap pool, leisure pool, hydroslide, lazy river, and deep water swim lanes. For those looking to unwind, there's a spa zone featuring a sauna, steam room, and hot pools. Whether you're cooling off with a splash, zooming down the hydroslide, or soaking in the spa to soothe your aching muscles, there's something for everyone. Not swimming? No problem. There's also a calm outdoor relaxation area beside the pool where students can unwind and hang out with friends under staff supervision.

Key Information:

After having some lunch we will leave Adrenalin Forest and walk to Te Rauparaha Arena, where students have use of the whole of the main pool, spas, sauna, steam room, hydro-slide and deep water swimming lane. If students are not swimming there is an outside area off the side of the main pool where they can relax.

>>

The rest of the group will walk back to Porirua station and catch the 2.39pm train back to Tawa Station where they will be dismissed.

Escape to the Outdoors!

Camp Wainui is a scenic outdoor facility located on the edge of the Remutaka Forest Park, offering a peaceful bush setting with wide open spaces and scenic surroundings. The site features a large grassed camping area, native forest tracks, and access to a river nearby. Facilities include a dining room, kitchen, segregated bathrooms, and plenty of tenting space, providing a balance between comfort and the classic, kiwi camping experience. The camp offers a wide range of outdoor activities such as raft building, bivvy making, search and rescue challenges, and bushcraft skills — perfect for learning, adventure, and team-building!
